Standing Rules of Order

Standing Rules of Order #

0.0 # Order of Business

  1. 0.0.1 # Reading and approval of minutes
  2. 0.0.2 # Reports of Officers and Standing Committees
  3. 0.0.3 # Reports of Special Committees
  4. 0.0.4 # Special Orders
  5. 0.0.5 # Business arising from reports of Standing Committees or Special Committees
  6. 0.0.6 # Unfinished Business and General Orders
  7. 0.0.7 # New Business

0.1 # Standing

  1. 0.1.1 # A member has the floor when recognized by the Speaker, and does not have to stand up.

0.2 # In-Camera Session

  1. 0.2.1 # The motion to enter an in camera session (or to “move in camera”) is a privileged motion that:
    1. 0.2.1.a # takes precedence of all others except the privileged motions to take a recess, to adjourn, and to fix the time to which to adjourn, to which it yields;
    2. 0.2.1.b # requires a majority vote;
    3. 0.2.1.c # is in order only when not already in camera;
    4. 0.2.1.d # is amendable only as to the additional persons permitted to remain during the in camera session;
    5. 0.2.1.e # may be renewed as frequently as the motion to adjourn;
    6. 0.2.1.f # requires no second;
    7. 0.2.1.g # is debatable only as to the anticipated need for secrecy;
    8. 0.2.1.h # cannot be reconsidered; and
    9. 0.2.1.i # is out of order when another has the floor.
  2. 0.2.2 # The motion to leave an in camera session (or to “move ex camera”) is a privileged motion that:
    1. 0.2.2.a # takes precedence of all others except the motion to enter an in camera session and those to which that motion yields, to which it yields;
    2. 0.2.2.b # requires a majority vote;
    3. 0.2.2.c # is in order only when made in camera;
    4. 0.2.2.d # is not amendable;
    5. 0.2.2.e # may be renewed as frequently as the motion to adjourn;
    6. 0.2.2.f # requires no second;
    7. 0.2.2.g # is debatable only as to the continued need (or lack thereof) for secrecy;
    8. 0.2.2.h # cannot be reconsidered; and
    9. 0.2.2.i # is out of order when another has the floor.
  3. 0.2.3 # The members of the assembly who are permitted to vote on the secret business to be discussed in camera, and the chair and secretary of the meeting, are always permitted to remain in an in camera session.
    1. 0.2.3.a # If it is desired to exclude a voting member of the assembly, they must first be barred from voting on the secret business.
    2. 0.2.3.b # If it is desired to exclude the chair or secretary, an interim chair or secretary must first be elected.
  4. 0.2.4 # If the motion to enter an in camera session is adopted:
    1. 0.2.4.a # when another question is before the assembly, the in camera session automatically ends when the pending main motion is disposed of, if not already ended;
    2. 0.2.4.b # otherwise, the in camera session ends only by motion.
  5. 0.2.5 # When a motion to enter an in camera session is adopted:
    1. 0.2.5.a # All persons not permitted to remain must leave the room.
    2. 0.2.5.b # The chair shall then prompt for a motion to leave the in camera session, in case the reasons for moving in camera were, themselves, secret.
  6. 0.2.6 # When a motion to leave an in camera session is adopted:
    1. 0.2.6.a # The in camera session ends immediately.
    2. 0.2.6.b # Any persons previously required to leave should be invited to return.
  7. 0.2.7 # Minutes are still kept during in camera sessions, but they shall not normally be published, and members permitted to remain are obliged to not divulge discussions made in camera.
    1. 0.2.7.a # Notwithstanding the above, a motion disposed of in camera must have its text and disposition published, unless its text was never publicized ex camera and implementing its effects does not require disclosing its disposition.
  8. 0.2.8 # These standing rules on in camera sessions replace the provisions of Robert’s Rules relating to executive sessions.
  9. 0.2.9 # While the correct Latin pronunciation of camera is /ˈkamera/, in the Engineering Society the word is pronounced [kʰ(ə)ˈmɛɹə], for historical reasons.

0.3 # Speaking Limits

  1. 0.3.1 # Members are permitted to speak any number of times and for any duration to a single motion, unless, by motion, the assembly adopts limits on debate.

0.4 # Submission of Documents

  1. 0.4.1 # All documents, reports, and appendices shall be submitted at least seventy-two (72) hours prior to the commencement of each regular meeting of the Board of Directors, unless otherwise specified in the Bylaws or barring extraneous circumstances at the discretion of the Speaker.
